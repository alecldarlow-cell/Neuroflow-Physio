The Science

Why exercise is medicine for the brain

These classes aren't adapted general fitness. They're built on the same neuroscience underpinning leading specialist rehabilitation programmes.

01

Parkinson's & High-Intensity Exercise

Strong clinical evidence shows that vigorous, high-amplitude exercise can slow the progression of Parkinson's symptoms. Research from PD Warrior and LSVT BIG demonstrates measurable improvements in movement amplitude, gait and balance with regular participation.

02

Neuroplasticity at Every Age

The nervous system retains the ability to rewire — neuroplasticity — at any age and at any stage post-neurological injury. Targeted, repetitive movement therapy actively stimulates new neural pathways, supporting functional recovery well beyond initial rehabilitation.

03

Pilates & Multiple Sclerosis

Pilates-based trunk control and core stability work has been shown in multiple studies to significantly improve balance scores, reduce fatigue impact and improve quality of life in people with MS. It is among the most recommended supplemental therapies for the condition.

04

Falls Prevention — What Works

NICE clinical guidelines recommend physiotherapist-led balance and strength training as first-line falls prevention. Group-based programmes consistently show the strongest adherence rates and long-term outcomes in preventing repeat falls.

Methodologies

Evidence-based frameworks in use

PD Warrior®

An internationally recognised high-intensity exercise programme for Parkinson's disease, based on principles of neuroplasticity and high-amplitude movement. Clinical evidence demonstrates improvements in movement amplitude, gait speed and quality of life.

LSVT BIG Principles

Lee Silverman Voice Treatment BIG trains people with Parkinson's to make bigger movements. Evidence shows participants achieve significant improvements in gait, balance and functional arm movement after treatment.

APPI Pilates Method

The Australian Physiotherapy and Pilates Institute method modifies classical Pilates exercises using the latest research on lumbar instability, muscle imbalance and neural tension — making Pilates safe and effective for people with neurological conditions.

PWR! Moves

Parkinson Wellness Recovery — a neuro-rehabilitation approach developed from LSVT BIG research. Task-specific exercises targeting Parkinson's-specific movement deficits, incorporated into the Parkinson's Pilates class.

Common Questions

Frequently asked

Do I need a GP referral to attend?

No referral is needed. You can contact us directly. Some clients do come via their GP, neurologist or NHS physiotherapist — and we're happy to liaise with your clinical team if helpful.

What if I have a bad day or a relapse?

Neurological conditions vary. If you're having a bad day, Chloe will adapt the session accordingly — or it may be best to rest. Blocks never expire, so you won't lose any sessions.

Can my carer or family member come with me?

Yes. Carers and family members are welcome to attend as observers, particularly for the Parkinson's Pilates class. Just let us know when you enquire and we'll accommodate this.

I use a wheelchair / walking aid. Can I still attend?

Absolutely. All classes can be adapted for people who use mobility aids or require chair-based exercise. The assessment will allow Chloe to plan the right modifications for you.

Can private health insurance cover sessions?

Some policies do cover physiotherapy-led exercise classes. We recommend checking with your insurer. We can provide receipts and session notes to support any insurance claim.

What if I've never done Pilates before?

No prior Pilates experience is needed or expected. All classes are built from the ground up for people with neurological conditions — not adapted from general fitness Pilates. Chloe will explain everything.

Is there parking and accessibility?

Classes are held at accessible venues in Norwich with step-free access and parking nearby. Full details including the exact venue address are provided when you book.

How quickly will I see results?

Many participants notice improvements in confidence and specific movements within 4–6 sessions. Sustained neurological benefit builds over months of regular attendance — consistency is key.
